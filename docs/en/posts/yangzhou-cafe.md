# Yangzhou Coffee Shop Work Guide

Based in Yangzhou, I code from coffee shops regularly. I've tested multiple spots for WiFi, outlets, noise, and coffee quality. Yangzhou has a slow pace, beautiful lakes and greenery — perfect for focused work.

<!-- more -->

## Evaluation Criteria

For remote workers, a good coffee shop has four dimensions:

| Dimension | Weight | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| WiFi Speed | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Download ≥ 50Mbps, upload ≥ 10Mbps |
| Outlets | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | No outlets = time-limited session, at least near the desk |
| Noise | ⭐⭐⭐ | Quiet for calls, tolerable for coding |
| Endurance | ⭐⭐⭐ | How long you can stay without being rushed out |

## Independent Cafés

### Jishe Cafe&Bar (Gengzi Street)

Day coffee, night bar model — one of the earliest in Yangzhou. The barista doubles as bartender, and specialty drinks have a cocktail-like complexity.

- **Address**: No. 66 Gengzi Street, Guangling District
- **Hours**: 12:00-18:00 / 19:00-24:00
- **Outlets**: Available, limited quantity
- **Noise**: Low, quiet in the afternoon
- **Recommended**: For those who want specialty drinks while working
- **Note**: Coffee is on the lighter side, ask for extra shots when ordering

### RIFF COFFEE (Jianzhen Store)

Camping-themed decor with canvas canopies, egg-roll tables, and camping chairs. Besides coffee, they serve homemade fruit vinegar. Owner is an outdoor enthusiast.

- **Address**: Building M, Vanke Rainbow Plaza, Hanjiang District
- **Hours**: 08:00-19:00
- **Outlets**: Available
- **Noise**: Low
- **Recommended**: For camping lovers, opens early
- **Note**: Irregular rest days, check if open before going

### Banjian Coffee (Songcheng Mingdu)

Inspired by Yangzhou's canals, with a real wooden boat decoration inside. The owner creates seasonal specialty drinks using regional ingredients and explains each one.

- **Address**: No. 77 Xinguyi Lane, Guangling District
- **Hours**: 09:00-18:00
- **Outlets**: Available
- **Noise**: Low, there's a cat and a dog in the store
- **Recommended**: For creative drink lovers who need a quiet environment
- **Note**: The shop dog goes for walks and sometimes brings back customers

### Zhouji Coffee

Self-roasted beans, constantly rotating menu, using SOE for coffee. Small but well-zoned: bar counter, Japanese-style seating, rooftop, and outdoor seats.

- **Address**: No. 13-9 Yanfu East Road, Guangling District
- **Hours**: 08:00-20:00
- **Outlets**: Available
- **Noise**: Medium-low
- **Recommended**: Coffee lovers who enjoy trying different beans
- **Note**: Limited seating, may not find a spot during peak hours

### TODAY'S BEAN

Self-roasted beans with a rich menu — there's always something to match your taste. Small and cozy, perfect for long sessions.

- **Address**: No. 41 Guan Alley, Guangling District
- **Hours**: 08:00-18:00
- **Outlets**: Available
- **Noise**: Low
- **Recommended**: Top pick for long work sessions
- **Note**: Small space, go early to secure a seat

## Chain Brands

### Starbucks (Various Locations)

Not many locations in Yangzhou, but basic amenities are available.

- **WiFi**: 30-60Mbps
- **Outlets**: About half the stores have them
- **Noise**: High, especially mall locations
- **Recommended**: Choose non-mall stores for better environment
- **Note**: Regular stores are nothing special, use as backup

### Manner

Great value. Americano at ¥15, ¥5 off with your own cup.

- **WiFi**: 30-50Mbps
- **Outlets**: New stores mostly have them
- **Noise**: Medium
- **Recommended**: Budget-conscious remote workers
- **Note**: Limited seating, hard to find a spot during peak hours

### Luckin Coffee

Cheap, convenient, widespread. But work experience is mediocre.

- **WiFi**: 20-40Mbps
- **Outlets**: Few
- **Noise**: Medium-high
- **Recommended**: Grab a coffee for an hour, not suitable for long sessions
- **Note**: Seating is leisure-oriented, not great for extended work

## Specialty Spaces

### Zhuozhuo Sansan Café (Dayun Tower)

Located on the first floor of the China Grand Canal Museum's Dayun Tower, with maximum art atmosphere. Equipped with a professional Italian LaMarzocco espresso machine.

- **Address**: First Floor, Dayun Tower, China Grand Canal Museum
- **WiFi**: Unknown, museum area likely has public WiFi
- **Outlets**: Unknown
- **Noise**: Low, museum environment is quiet
- **Recommended**: For those who want to work in an art space
- **Note**: Need to visit the museum first, suitable for half-day tour + work

## What to Avoid

### Skip These Types

- **Mall cafés**: Noisy, slow WiFi, uncomfortable seating
- **Instagram spots**: More photographers than workers, noisy environment
- **Delivery-only shops**: No seating or extremely uncomfortable

### Best Time Slots

| Time | Rating | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| 9:00-11:00 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Quiet, seats available |
| 11:00-14:00 | ⭐⭐ | Lunch rush, crowded and noisy |
| 14:00-17:00 | ⭐⭐⭐ | Getting busier, still okay |
| 17:00-19:00 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Gap before evening rush |
| After 19:00 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Empty, but some places close early |

### Essential Gear

- **Noise-canceling headphones**: The #1 productivity tool for café work
- **Power bank**: Lifesaver when outlets are scarce
- **Mouse pad**: Some tables are too smooth, mouse slides
- **Phone hotspot**: Plan B when WiFi dies

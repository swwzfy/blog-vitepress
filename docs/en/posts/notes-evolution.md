# My Notes System Evolution

From Apple Notes to Notion to Obsidian, then to my own system. The process of tinkering with note tools is itself a form of thinking.

<!-- more -->

## Phase 1: Apple Notes

Simple, fast sync, search is enough. The problem is once notes pile up, organization relies entirely on "folders" — not flexible enough.

## Phase 2: Notion

Database views are powerful. But after six months, two issues made me quit: slow loading, and my data wasn't in my hands.

## Phase 3: Obsidian

Markdown + local files = peace of mind. Bidirectional links, graph view, community plugins — used it for two years, the most satisfying solution.

Until I realized I was spending 40% of my "note time" tweaking plugins and templates...

## Now

Obsidian for daily notes, important knowledge synced to my own SQLite database via scripts, with vector search.

> The best note system is the one you actually use.
